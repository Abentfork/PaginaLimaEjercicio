Meet Julian Corrie

Julian Corrie, known for his electronic music background and work as a keyboardist and guitarist in Franz Ferdinand, shares his experiences in the band, his influences, and his creative process.

Julian Corrie

Role: Keyboards, Synths

1. How did you transition from electronic music to joining Franz Ferdinand?

I've always been passionate about music, whether electronic or rock. I had my own project, Miaoux Miaoux, which was more synth-based. When I joined Franz Ferdinand, it felt like an exciting new challenge—blending electronic elements into their sound.

2. What’s it like working with the band?

It’s been amazing. Everyone is super creative, and the energy in the studio and on stage is incredible. The chemistry between us makes everything flow naturally.

3. What instruments and synths do you use most frequently?

I love using vintage synths, especially analog ones like the Juno-60. But I also enjoy experimenting with modern digital synths to create unique textures.

4. How do you approach songwriting and composing?

It depends on the mood. Sometimes I start with a synth melody, sometimes with a beat. I love layering sounds to create a dynamic and immersive atmosphere.
